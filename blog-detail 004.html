How did I prepare for TOPIK I Test (1–2급)?

June 01, 2017

What is TOPIK?

The TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) was first conducted once in 1997. It is a test to evaluate the ability of non-native speakers for expression and comprehension in the Korean language. It measures proficiency in reading, writing, and listening comprehension. The test has three different levels: Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced, but it divided scores into six grades. To elaborate,
Levels 1 and 2 – Beginner Level
Levels 3 and 4 – Intermediate Level
Levels 5 and 6 – Advanced Level
In TOPIK-I (Level 1-2), it has just two sections – Reading and Listening. In TOPIK II (Level 3-6), it has 3 sections – Reading, Listening and Writing. And you can take both TOPIK I and II on the same day if you want to.

Here are my study tips on how I prepared for the TOPIK test.


1. Always use the plain or formal/polite form of sentence-ending

Never use the casual spoken form (아요/어요) or lower form (아/어) of language and do not mix two forms (e.g. – ㅂ니다/습니다 and 아요/어요). Just always write (다/ㄴ다/는다) Or (ㅂ니다/습니다).


2. Focus on 여자 and 남자 characters in listening test

In the listening section, questions usually ask what the 남자 or 여자is saying in the conversation. So, you need to read the instructions carefully and focus on 남자 or 여자 part according to what is being asked.


3. Don’t bring anything to Exam Hall except the admit card or exam-taking ID

Leave all the notes and books behind at home. Just wait to hear the start bell. Last time study of vocabulary and grammar will make you no good, so just wait to hear the start bell to go into the exam room. Probably they will not allow any stationary into the exam hall. You have to colour the OMR answer sheet with a special pen that they will provide you. If you make any mistake and want to change your answer, you just have to raise your hand and the invigilator will come to you and remove the wrong mark with a correction pen.


4. Self-study is the most important things

There is no single strategy that works for everyone. You need to know which strategy works best for you. Sometimes solving a particular section or type of questions first can make a big difference.


5. Make a strategy in advance to solve questions!

I recommend you to watch K-dramas, films, reality shows, and news without English subtitles, but if you are just a beginner, you can watch it with English subtitles. This is a way to make you familiar with Korean words and phrases. And you can just play Korean drama while you were doing something like cooking or exercising even if you are not watching them. BUT!!! you need to listen to what the actors and actresses are saying. This is a way of learning Korean for me when I don’t have time to watch Korean drama.


6. Make a collection of TOPIK Practice Tests

Practice tests are not only your guidebooks but also your teachers. Taking them a lot will enable you to find out your weak points in grammar and vocabulary.


7. Search for your weak points

Don’t be sad even if you get the lowest score. You have time until the exam to correct your mistakes. Remember, it is okay to make mistake while practising, but not in the exam.


8. Never repeat listening audio

Don’t ever rewind listening audio if you miss a point. You can listen to it again and again while checking your answers. This helps you a lot to concentrate on the listening part.


9. Make a list of Grammar Pattern and Vocabularies

After solving a TOPIK test, you must make a list of grammar patterns or vocabularies that are new to you or you don’t understand. Also, write down the explanation in your interpretation and revise it daily.


10. Have patience and remain focused in Reading Section!

For people who don’t like reading, it might make you feel dizzy. Even if your poor tired brain will start persuading you to give up, you need to keep concentrated for just a few more minutes because it can change your TOPIK score significantly.
